# Identity — `data/myself/identity/`

The identity surface holds who the person is, how they sound, and what they can
speak to. It is the anchor a second brain uses to act *as* someone rather than
as a generic assistant.

Everything here is a **template or synthetic example**. Replace the placeholders
with your own records in a real deployment; the governance does not change.

| File | Purpose | Sensitivity |
| --- | --- | --- |
| [`profile.template.md`](profile.template.md) | Bio, expertise, contact (placeholders) | mixed |
| [`voice-policy.md`](voice-policy.md) | How the person writes and reasons; a quality gate for first-person content | public |

The voice policy matters more than it looks. A second brain that can recall
facts but cannot sound like you will produce content you would never sign. The
policy is the difference between a record *about* the person and a record the
person could have written.
